    Guiding Emotions to Close More Sales

     

    Let's talk seriously about “manipulating” a person's emotions and decision making strategy. 

     

    First of all, believe in what you are doing so you can be of one mind about it.  Remember, you are helping this person.  In fact, with a smoker - chances are you are saving his or her life. 

     

    Every cigarette a person smokes subtracts 14 minutes from their life span.  We all manipulate one another every day, but in this case you are doing it for their own good and many of them will be grateful to you for the rest of their lives. 

     

    So feel good about it and let any guilt go!  It's GOOD to be manipulative if you are saving their lives or permanently improving the quality of their lives.  And also they WANT you to convince them to do it or they wouldn't have called in the first place.  They want you to help them let go of their fears and commit.

     

    So, as you talk to a lead always keep in mind:  "What do I want him to feel" and aim your questions towards eliciting those feelings.  For instance:

     

    Aim for him to feel these feelings (basically in this order):

     

    1.       Trust in you as a person and that you are telling him the truth

    2.       Motivated to move away from problems of the past

    3.       Motivated to succeed by focusing on the benefits to him

    4.       Belief that the program works and that he is in good hands

    5.       Belief that he has nothing to lose because of guarantee and overall money savings

    6.       Fear of loss if he doesn't book now

    7.       Feeling of being decisive

     

     

    The way to elicit these feelings are to:

     

    A.    Ask questions that remind them of past moments where he felt that way

    B.     Tell stories that inspire those feelings

    C.     Make comparisons with similar things that inspire those feelings

    D.    Ask questions or describe future scenes that elicit those feelings

     

    NEVER ask questions that engender negative feelings unless you IMMEDIATELY follow it with something that gives them a solution to those bad feelings.  Also never ask a question that instills a question about our effectiveness. 

     

    For instance “have you ever tried hypnosis before” is a terrible question to ask because it implies that it won't work because if he had then obviously it didn't work.  Don’t dwell on their past failures either when they mention them because it will only make them feel more like a failure and that doesn’t help you.  Only bring them up if you are going to immediately offer us a solution and strengthen those feelings of confidence!

                                     

    A nice tip is to use lots of sensory imagery and especially using the individuals favorite sense if you’ve noticed what that is.  For instance if he says “I SEE what you mean” (visual) then use words like “when you LOOK AT THE BIG PICTURE.”  Or if he says “I am just a little UNCOMFORTABLE” (sense of touch) then say “I SENSE that… what would make you FEEL better about it?”  That kind of thing.  But in the absence of that knowledge, just be vivid with several senses when you can.

     

    Let’s look at the feelings you want to engender and ways to do it:

     

    1.       Feel Trust in you as a person and that you are telling him the truth.

     

    a.       Be yourself

    b.      Imagine you can see this person – what he looks like.  It doesn’t matter if it’s accurate, just as long as it’s positive.  Pretend you are talking to an old and very dear friend.  It will echo in your voice.

    c.       TELL THE TRUTH – You don’t need to lie to anyone.  (You DO need to focus on POSITIVES though)  We really do succeed the vast majority of the time.  In the few cases we do fail it is because the client is not ready – but when they ARE ready they can come back free of charge for life (except weight loss and neurofeedback programs – and even here our programs are VERY fair and reasonable)

    d.      Say something they don’t expect – or pace them.  Like if they object to the price you can laugh and say “I know!  I freaked out the first time I heard the price.”

    This is called “Pacing.”  You Pace their objection and look at it from their point of view first.  Remember:  NO OBJECTION IS UNREASONABLE FROM THEIR POINT OF VIEW!  When you respect their point of view instead of arguing with them then you become friends and they will listen to you because you are listening to them.  But don’t over pace… don’t let them draw you into their belief system too much – Just remember… the way they believe has FAILED THEM UTTERLY or they wouldn’t be calling.  Pace enough to be respectful and understanding and then…

    LEAD them in a more reasonable point of view that will allow them to really live their values and say something like “But then as I learned more about it and saw all the successes they had… and when I realized how much people spend on cigarettes – I realized that it’s more than fair.  Do you realize a pack-a-day smoker pays $1200 every year for cigs?  That’s scary isn’t it?  $495 is nothing compared to what you’ve BEEN spending and what you will SAVE in the future… it’s free really when you do that math right?  I mean… let alone the health benefits… etc.”

     

    e.       Tell the truth about other things that show you aren’t just a “drone” telling scripts but a real person.  Break rules a little about what people expect if it feels right to do so.  When you show that the truth is important to you and you are willing to say things that are outside of the box then people will assume that you are being honest about everything.

     

    2.       Feel motivated to move away from problems of the past

     

    a.       Ask “If you don’t mind my asking, what (presupposition) health problems have you been suffering from or how has the habit affected their life negatively.  Why have you decided (presupposition) to MAKE THIS CHOICE now?”

     

    b.      Listen… ask questions that help you understand why that matters to them. (“Do you have kids or grandkids” is a good one… “So you’ve been in the hospital” “So your doctor suggested it”  “So it’s really effected your self esteem” “That roller coaster of dieting and then gaining again is awful isn’t it? etc.) and then…

     

    3.       Feel motivated to succeed by focusing on the future benefits to him

     

    a.       Congratulate them:  “Great.  Well, first I’d like to honestly say congratulations on making the decision to call and take care of this – it’s a big deal to step up to the bat and face this head on.  Too many other people make excuses and run away from their problems.  I really respect you for this.” (and you honestly should!)

     

    b.      Then Echo back what they say to you on the positive side:  For instance if they say they have had health problems you can paint a picture of what it will be like once they’ve succeed:  “Yeah, it will be nice to have the extra energy to be able to keep up with the kids’ won’t it?”  “To be able to stop that emphasema and breath more easily will be a Godsend for you” or “Remember what it’s like for the men to turn around to look at you as you walk by in a bathing suit – won’t that be nice to be able to have that again?” 

    Paint a picture, remind them of what it feels like and the words they will be able to tell themselves when they succeed.  If you can get them thinking about what it will feel like to succeed then they will actually feel those feelings in anticipation.  First you make them want it.

     

    The rule of thumb is THE STRONGEST EMOTION WINS!  If they feel safe with you and are excited about the program and want what you have them imagining – then they will book.  (If fear of loss is stronger – you would lose it)

     

    c.       Rattle off exactly what the program will accomplish for them. 

    For smoking:

    ·         We’re going to increase your motivation

    ·         Increase your confidence in your ability to quit

    ·         We’ll teach you how to reduce your stress level and

    ·         And it will either make it a WHOLE lot easier for you to choose to quit or

    ·         For the MAJORITY of people it will remove all cravings entirely where you may very well just forget to smoke.  It can be that automatic and easy since we are directly re-programming the habits in your subconscious mind.

     

    For Weight Loss:

    ·         We're going to help you with portion control

    ·         making better choices

    ·         stopping any emotional eating you may be doing

    ·         end any sugar [or other] cravings you may have

    ·         Other bullet points can be "end the war you've been having with yourself over this" and "increase your motivation."


     

    4.       Feel confident that the program works and that he is in good hands


    If it sounds like he is feeling good - This is the time to first try and close. 

     

    *ALWAYS TRY AND CLOSE WHEN YOU CAN TELL THEY ARE FEELING GOOD!  Timing is EVERYTHING when it comes to closing.  Always listen to their tone of voice!

     

    ONLY try to close when they sound positive.

    ALWAYS try to close when they sound positive!

     

    And remember, sometimes they call excited and ready already.  If so BOOK THEIR APPOINTMENT!  Don’t waste time selling them if they are ready to book – the only thing you could accomplish is talking them out of it!  If you aren’t sure ask “are you ready to book now, or what questions do you have?”

     

    To Close the Sale:

     

    Explain the process of BOOKING first!  This assumes that he believes in us (and he has no reason not to) and that we believe in ourselves and it’s just a matter of booking an appointment. 

     

    a.       Tell him the price matter-of-factly, “The total price of the program is _________.  First we ask for a $100 non-refundable deposit to hold the appointment for you.”

     

    b.      “Then we’ll set up your appointment and you can choose to see either a male or a female therapist” (giving them more double-bind choices/presuming sale) and then

     

    c.       “once we have that set up we will be sending you out a questionnaire.  Now this questionnaire is very important because it will ask you about all your triggers for your habit and that will give your therapist a lot of the information he or she will need to help you eliminate them.  You can bring it back in with you, but you might want to fax it in or mail it in to us in advance which will give your therapist more time to look it over before you come in.  Okay?  (elicits an implied agreement to come it…)

     

    d.      Next ask:  Does a morning or afternoon appointment work best for you?
    This is the moment of truth – you’ve just asked for the sale and he’s setting an appointment at this point.  If he has any objections then he will bring them up now for you to pace and lead them until he is once again excited, past his fears and ready for you to try and close him again.  Wait until you have him excited!

     

    e.       Then you say “all I need now is a credit card to hold the appointment for you.  Will you be using Visa or MasterCard?” (again, a double bind – “not paying” is not a choice) 

     

    5.       Belief that he has nothing to lose because of guarantee and overall money savings

     

    Money is one of people’s initial objections, but it’s not the real one.  The real objections are “will it work?” “Do I trust this person?” etc.

     

    6.       Fear of loss if he doesn't book now

     

    A good strategy is to say “right now we’re booking a month out” (which we usually are..) “but I can get you in on_____ (day) with _______(therapist).”  Creates a sense of urgency to help them commit.

     

    7.       Feeling of being decisive

     

    The sense of urgency helps with this, and you don’t want to be too pushy.  If you’ve established real rapport with them, sending them a brochure and giving them time will enable them to overcome their fears and commit.

     

    You might also ask questions along the lines of “I don’t want to sound pushy or pressure you.  I just want you to do what is right for you –whatever that is.  The only advice I would give now is when you think about whether to DO THIS NOW the questions to ask yourself are ‘what are my values?’ (pause) ‘What really matters most?’ (pause) ‘what am I afraid of’ and ‘What it will be like when I DO STEP UP TO THE PLATE (or commit)… and succeed once and for all?’ (pause)” 

     

    Then maybe back off a little and say “I won’t set an appointment for you now (taking control like it’s YOUR choice and putting them in a position where they again feel like they might be missing an opportunity – which they are) – but I am going to send you some information to read so you can learn more about this and make your own decision to come in.  What’s your address?”

     

     

    Also VERY IMPORTANT:  Don’t just pepper them with questions.  Stop regularly and respond to their answers as you go.  LISTEN.  Otherwise they will feel uncomfortable.  Relate to the person and have a conversation with them, not a monologue.  Always keep control of the conversation and steer it where it needs to go for their own good, but don’t EVER sound like you are reading a script. 

     

    LISTEN TO THEM AND HEAR WHAT THEIR NEEDS ARE AND THEN SHOW THEM HOW WE CAN HONESTLY HELP THEM MEET THEM!!!  And you will make more money and change the world one person at a time!
